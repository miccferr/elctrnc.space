Burglar Alarm DIY kit, sold in various configurations and under different brands e.g.

I bought around 2017 as Burglar Alarm DIY Kit Burglar Alarm Announciator Electronic Production Suite from Ebay. Fancy name. The product is now discontinued, the manifacturer’s website on the PCB (www.yqd1y.cn) seems to be not existing.

The kit can still be purchased under a different brand here for around 5 quid.

BOM:

  • 2 kOhm
  • 1 200kOhm
  • 1 MOhm br/bl/gr/slv
  • 4 s9014 c331 transistors
  • 1 3mm LED
  • 1 “103” 10nF Capacitor
  • 1 “104” 100nF Capacitor
  • 4 15cm (1mm) red/black electric cables
  • 4 plastic-y pin adapter for the cables
  • 1 8Ohm 0.25W Loudspeaker branded “ROHS”

Terms:

-NPN transistor = Negative-Postive-Negative configuration of a BJT transitor. Positive current flows from the Collector to the Emitter.

  • BJT= Bipolar Junction Transitor.
  • hFE = is the amplification factor that a current receives when flowing from a the Base to the Collector or Emitter link
  • Audio amplifier = electronic device that amplifies low-power electronic audio signals enough to drive a loudspeaker. It is limited by the electrical energy it can output.
  • Loudspeaker = Electroacustic Transducer. The thing with vibrating cones that transform electric signal into audio waves. Limited by the electrical energy it can convert into sound without destroying the cones.
  • Audio Power = is the electrical power transfered from an Audio Amplifier to a Loudspeaker
  • RMS Power = The Audio Power emitted by an Audio amplifiers is measured as Continuous Average sine wave power ratings. Wikipedia tells that average power refers to the avgerage value of the instantaneous power waveform over time. As this is typically calculated as Root Mean Square error (RMS) of the sine wave voltage, it’s misleading referred to, often by manifacturers and even CE regulations as “RMS Power”. But this is not really the power itself, rather the error. Still being a larger number I guess it made Amplifiers look more “powerful”. Read the full story here.
  • Capacitor = NOTE: often referred to as Condensator. A device that store electrical energy in an electric. It’s used to add Capacitance to a circuit.
  • Capacitance (C) = Measured in Farad, C = A^2 _ s^4 _ Kg^-1 * m^-2 = charge/voltage
  • Passive = an element that consumes but doesn’t produce energy. Or an element incapable of Power Gain, i.e. incapable of amplifying a signal.
  • 103 Capacitor = First 2 digits: 10 + Third digit (num of 00s)= 10 +000 = 10000 pF = 1.10e4 pico-Farad = 10 nano-Farad
